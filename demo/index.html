Historical Calibration & Modeling Framework

Aethel’s economic presets are calibrated on historical data spanning 2005 to the present. This timeframe captures distinct macroeconomic cycles, including the 2008 Global Financial Crisis, the post-crisis quantitative easing cycle, and the global inflationary spikes of 2021–2023.

1. Equities Model

Merton Jump-Diffusion

Modeled via log-return transitions utilizing global benchmarks (MSCI World ETF / URTH). Automatically accounts for splits, dividends, and sudden discontinuous downward asset price jumps.

2. Interest Rate Model

Cox-Ingersoll-Ross (CIR)

Mathematical short-rate model parameterized to the Effective Federal Funds Rate (FRED) as a global risk-free rate proxy, utilizing a square-root process (σ√rt) to enforce non-negative nominal yields.

3. Inflation Model

Ornstein-Uhlenbeck

Mean-reverting stochastic model fitted directly to the rolling 12-Month Year-on-Year change of the G7 CPI baseline index (CPIAUCSL proxy).

Expert Parameters (World Baseline)

  • Annualized Frictional Drag: 25 bps portfolio-wide maintenance fees applied monthly.
  • Capital Gains Taxation: 15% flat rate on gains, calculated dynamically during portfolio rebalancing and distributions.
  • Rate & Deflation Floors: Short-rate proxy restricted to ≥ 0.1% to prevent mathematically invalid negative roots.
